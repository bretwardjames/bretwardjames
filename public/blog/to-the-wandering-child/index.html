To the wandering child

Posted on Dec 20, 2006

O thou wanderer Thine heart in doubt, Whither thou goest Knowest thou not? This path that thou treadest, With darkness beset, Leadeth not to that rest, Which thou wishest to get.

O thou wanderer, Thou goest to and fro Searching here and searching there, But for what, thou dost not know. O beloved daughter, Seest thou the end? Treacherous is the resolution Of that trail which you descend.

O thou wanderer, Wilt thou suffer long? Come with me, I pray thee And listen to my song. O dearest child Wilt thou not sing? Wist ye not that thou Art a child of the King?
